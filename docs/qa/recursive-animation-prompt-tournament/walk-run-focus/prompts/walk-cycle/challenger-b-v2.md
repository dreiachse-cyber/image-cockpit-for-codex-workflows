# walk-cycle Challenger B v2 prompt

Challenger B2 is a walk-only refinement after the ranger same-source A/B rerun.
It keeps Champion A's calm grounded timing and imports only the useful B v1 foot-depth cues.

## Hypothesis

Champion A remains the official walk champion because B v1 became too jog-like on the ranger source.
B2 should improve front/back foot readability without adding running energy.

## Base prompt

8-frame calm grounded walk cycle with visible left-right foot alternation, two soft contact poses, two passing poses, subtle front/back shoe depth, small hip bob, low knee lift, short-to-moderate stride, opposite arm swing, stable body scale, full feet visible, stable ground line, one foot always planted, no airborne frame

## Additional contract lines

- Use the 8 generated source frames as one complete normal walk loop. Do not make a ping-pong half-cycle.
- Keep the motion calm, readable, and grounded. The character should look like they are walking at a relaxed RPG field-map pace, not jogging, marching, or sprinting.
- Frame 1: relaxed left-foot-forward contact with the right foot slightly back; both feet remain close enough to read as a walk.
- Frame 2: body weight settles gently over the left planted foot; do not deepen into a run compression pose.
- Frame 3: passing pose with both feet close under the hips, rear heel lifting slightly, knees only mildly bent.
- Frame 4: right foot makes a small forward reach and prepares heel-or-toe contact; keep the step compact.
- Frame 5: relaxed right-foot-forward contact with the left foot slightly back; the silhouette differs from frame 1 but stays modest.
- Frame 6: body weight settles gently over the right planted foot; the right foot remains visually planted.
- Frame 7: second passing pose with both feet close under the hips, rear heel lifting slightly, knees only mildly bent.
- Frame 8: left foot makes a small forward reach and bridges cleanly into frame 1 without duplicating frame 1 exactly.
- In front and back rows, show foot depth with a subtle shoe offset: the forward shoe can be a little lower or slightly larger, and the rear shoe can tuck slightly behind. Keep both legs readable below the tunic or cloak.
- In side and diagonal rows, keep at least one sole or toe visually touching the same ground line in every frame. No flight frame.
- Keep torso bob and hip shift smaller than B v1. The body should stay upright, with no strong forward lean.
- Arms swing opposite the legs but remain secondary and compact.

## Negative cues

Avoid running pose, jogging energy, long stride, strong forward lean, exaggerated crossover, airborne gap, high knee lift, deep crouch, skating feet, mirrored clothing-only motion, hidden knees, tiny static shuffle, duplicated frame 1/frame 5 silhouettes, cropped shoes, and body scale drift.
